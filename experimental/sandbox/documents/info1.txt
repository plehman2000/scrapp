Kamala Devi Harris[b] (born October 20, 1964) is an American politician and attorney who is the 49th and current vice president of the United States since 2021, serving with President Joe Biden. She is the first female vice president, making her the highest-ranking female official in U.S. history, as well as both the first African American and the first Asian American vice president.[3][4] She served as a U.S. senator from California from 2017 to 2021, and earlier 
as the attorney general of California. Harris is the Democratic Party's presidential nominee in the 2024 U.S. presidential election.[5][6]
